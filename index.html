12 Ways To Make Money Online

12 ways to make money on the internet, with detailed explanations and information on average salaries. Keep in mind that these values are indicative and can vary significantly based on factors such as experience, market demand, and dedicated effort.

1. Writing Freelancer (Copywriting and Content)

A writing freelancer is an independent professional who offers writing services independently, rather than being hired as a permanent employee by a company. These professionals work on their own, generally offering their services to a variety of clients, and can perform a variety of writing-related tasks, such as creating content for blogs, articles, advertising copy, technical writing, proofreading, among others.

Writing freelancers have the flexibility to choose the projects they want to work on, set their own hours, and set their own pay rates. They can be hired by companies, marketing agencies, individual business owners, or anyone who needs specialized writing skills for a specific period of time.

Writing freelancers need to be self-sufficient, manage their time efficiently, promote their services, and maintain good relationships with clients to ensure ongoing work opportunities. This type of work is common in several areas, especially in the field of writing and producing online content.

Average Salary: Initially, $20-$50 per hour; with experience, it can exceed $100 per hour.

2. Affiliate Marketing

Affiliate marketing is a marketing strategy in which a company rewards affiliates for each customer or visitor they direct to the company's website through the affiliates' marketing efforts. In other words, affiliates earn commissions for promoting third-party products or services and generating sales or leads.

The process generally works as follows:

  • Registering as an Affiliate: Individuals or companies register as affiliates of an affiliate marketing program.
  • Choosing Products or Services: Affiliates choose the products or services they want to promote, often receiving a unique affiliate link.
  • Promotion: Affiliates use their own marketing channels, such as blogs, websites, social media, emails, or other means, to promote the products or services.
  • Referral Tracking: The affiliate link contains a unique code that tracks when a customer clicks on it and takes a desired action, such as a purchase.
  • Generating Commissions: When customers make a purchase or take the desired action, affiliates receive a commission based on the rules of the affiliate program.

This strategy benefits all parties involved. Advertisers are able to increase their online presence and sales without spending money upfront on advertising. Affiliates have the opportunity to earn money by promoting products or services that are relevant to their target audience. And consumers can discover useful products or services through trusted sources.

Companies and entrepreneurs often use affiliation platforms to manage these programs, making it easier to track sales, pay commissions and manage affiliates.

Average Salary: Variable. It may start modest but can successfully generate thousands of dollars per month.

3. Create and Sell Online Courses

"Create and Sell Online Courses" refers to the process of developing educational courses and making them available on the internet so that interested parties can enroll, participate and acquire knowledge on a specific subject. This business model has become increasingly popular due to the ease of access to the internet and the demand for online education.

Here are some steps involved in the process of creating and selling online courses:

  • Identify the Niche or Topic: Choose a specific topic that you are knowledgeable in and that is relevant to your target audience. It could be anything from technical skills to broader subjects like personal development.
  • Plan Course Content: Structure your course content in a logical and progressive manner. Divide it into modules or lessons, creating a learning sequence.
  • Produce Multimedia Content: Create educational materials, such as videos, slide shows, texts, quizzes and exercises. Make sure your content is engaging and easy to understand.
  • Choosing a Hosting Platform: There are several online platforms that allow you to host and sell your courses, such as Udemy, Teachable, Coursera, among others. Choose the one that best meets your needs.
  • Set Up Pricing and Payments: Set the price for your course and choose payment options.
  • Marketing and Promotion: Promote your course to reach your target audience. This may include the use of social media, content marketing, email marketing, among other strategies.
  • Student Support: Offer support to students throughout the course. This could include a discussion forum, live Q&A sessions, or email support.
  • Updates and Improvements: Keep your course updated over time. If there is student feedback or changes to the field of study, make any necessary updates.

By following these steps, you can create a source of income online by selling educational courses. Remember that success in this business model requires not only knowledge on the subject, but also skills in marketing and managing online courses.

Average Salary: Depends on the niche and quality of the course. It can range from $500 to $5000 per month.

4. Development of Mobile Applications and Games

The development of mobile applications and games refers to the process of creating specific software for mobile devices, such as smartphones and tablets. This field spans a variety of platforms, including iOS (for Apple devices such as the iPhone and iPad) and Android (for a wide range of devices from different manufacturers).

Mobile app development involves creating software to perform specific tasks, such as social networking, productivity, health, entertainment, among others. These apps can be distributed through app stores such as Apple's App Store or Google Play Store.

On the other hand, mobile game development refers to the creation of electronic games designed to be played on mobile devices. These can range from simple, casual games to more complex and immersive experiences. Mobile games can include a variety of genres, such as puzzles, strategy, action, adventure, among others.

Developers use different programming languages and frameworks to create mobile apps and games. For iOS, the main language is Swift, while for Android, it is common to use Java or Kotlin. Additionally, there are cross-platform frameworks like Flutter and React Native that allow you to develop apps for both platforms using a single codebase.

The development of mobile applications and games is a dynamic and constantly evolving area, with new technologies and trends emerging regularly to improve the user experience and take advantage of the capabilities of mobile devices.

Average Salary: Initially, $30-$50 per hour; with successful projects, it can reach $100 per hour.

5. Digital Marketing (SEO, SEM, SMM)

Digital marketing refers to the use of marketing strategies that involve using online channels and platforms to promote products, services or brands. Unlike traditional marketing, which focuses on offline media, such as television, radio and print, digital marketing takes advantage of the internet and its various tools to reach the target audience in a more targeted and measurable way.

Some of the key components of digital marketing include:

  • Website and SEO: Having a website optimized for search engines (SEO) is essential to ensure that your online presence is easily found by users interested in your products or services.
  • Social Media: The use of platforms such as Facebook, Instagram, Twitter and LinkedIn to build relationships with audiences, promote content and increase brand visibility.
  • Content Marketing: The creation and distribution of relevant and valuable content to attract, engage and retain a target audience.
  • Email Marketing: Sending direct messages via email to a list of contacts, with the aim of promoting products, sharing relevant information or creating engagement.
  • Online Advertising: The use of paid advertisements on platforms such as Google Ads, Facebook Ads and others, to increase visibility and drive traffic to the website.
  • Influencer Marketing: Collaboration with influential people on social media to promote products or services.
  • Analytics and Monitoring: Use of analytical tools to monitor campaign performance, understand user behavior and adjust strategies as necessary.

Digital marketing offers the advantage of being highly measurable, allowing marketers to evaluate return on investment (ROI) and adjust their strategies based on real data.

Furthermore, it provides greater segmentation, making it possible to direct messages to specific audiences based on demographic, behavioral and geographic data.

Average Salary: $50,000 to $80,000 per year for beginners; up to $150,000 or more for experienced professionals.

6. Selling Products as an Affiliate

Affiliate selling refers to a business model in which you promote other companies' products or services and earn commissions for each sale made through your marketing efforts.

Affiliates do not need to create or manage products, handle inventory, or handle customer service. Instead, they focus their efforts on promoting the products and driving qualified traffic to the affiliate links.

The process generally works as follows:

  • Register as an Affiliate: You register as an affiliate in an affiliate program offered by a company.
  • Choice of Products: After registration, you choose the products or services you want to promote. Each product will have a unique affiliate link.
  • Marketing and Promotion: You use various marketing strategies, such as blogs, social media, email marketing, videos, among others, to promote products and drive traffic to affiliate links.
  • Sales Generation: When visitors click on your affiliate links and make a purchase, you earn a commission on the sale. Commissions can be a percentage of the sale value or a fixed amount.
  • Tracking and Payments: Affiliate programs often provide dashboards to track your sales and earnings. Accrued commissions are paid according to the terms of the program, which may be monthly, quarterly, or at other intervals.

Affiliate marketing is a popular form of monetization on the internet, as it allows affiliates to generate income without the need to create their own product. However, success in affiliate marketing often requires knowledge of digital marketing, understanding your target audience, and the ability to create persuasive and relevant content.

Average Salary: Depends on products and commissions, but can range from $100 to $1000 per month.

7. Graphic Design and Creative Freelancer

Graphic Design refers to the practice of visually creating communicative solutions through the use of visual elements such as images, typography, colors and layout. Graphic designers use their skills to convey messages effectively and aesthetically pleasing, whether in print or digital media. They work on a variety of projects, such as logos, flyers, websites, promotional materials, among others.

A Creative Freelancer, in turn, is an independent professional who offers graphic design services autonomously. Unlike graphic designers hired full-time by a company, freelancers have the flexibility to choose their projects, set their own hours, and work remotely. This allows them to serve a variety of clients and projects, often working for different companies or individuals over time.

Creative Freelancers can specialize in various areas of graphic design, such as visual identity, interface design, illustration, animation, among others. Your creativity and technical skills are your main assets, as you need to stand out in the competitive market, attract customers and deliver quality work. Furthermore, efficient time management, clear communication with clients and the ability to stay up to date with design trends are key aspects to success as a creative freelancer.

Average Salary: Initially, $20-$40 per hour; with experience, it can exceed $80 per hour.

8. Selling Photographs and Digital Art

Selling photographs and digital art refers to the process of marketing photographic images or digitally created works of art. This is a growing market, driven by increasing demand for visual content online, in areas such as interior design, social media, websites, advertising and more.

There are several ways to sell photographs and digital art:

  • Stock Platforms: Many photographers and digital artists choose to sell their work on stock platforms, such as Shutterstock, Adobe Stock, iStock, among others. These platforms allow creators to make their images available for purchase and download by a wide audience.
  • Personal Websites:Some artists prefer to create their own websites to sell their works. This gives you more control over the sales process, branding and direct interaction with customers.
  • Social Networks and Online Markets:Using social media, such as Instagram, Facebook or Etsy, to promote and sell your works is a popular approach. Many artists establish a solid online presence on these platforms to reach a wider audience.
  • Online Auctions:Some works of digital art are sold through online auctions, where interested parties place bids to purchase a specific piece.
  • Licensing:In addition to direct sales, many artists choose to license their images for use in advertising, products, graphic design, among others. This approach allows the same work to be used by multiple companies or individuals for royalty payments.
  • Events and Exhibitions:Attending local events, art shows, or fairs can also be an effective way to sell photographs and digital art, connecting directly with interested buyers.

When embarking on this market, it is important to consider legal issues, such as copyright and licensing, and create effective marketing strategies to promote your work and attract potential buyers. Furthermore, the technical and artistic quality of images plays a crucial role in customer satisfaction and sales success.

Average Salary: Varies, but can be from $50 to $500 per image, depending on demand and quality.

9. Dropshipping and E-Commerce

Dropshipping and E-Commerce are concepts related to e-commerce, but they refer to distinct approaches to operating an online business.

E-Commerce (Electronic Commerce):

  • E-Commerce, or electronic commerce, is a general term that encompasses any type of commercial transaction carried out over the internet.
  • It includes the buying and selling of products or services online, where consumers can place orders, make payments and receive products without needing to visit a physical store.
  • There are different E-Commerce models, such as B2C (Business-to-Consumer), B2B (Business-to-Business), C2C (Consumer-to-Consumer), among others.

Dropshipping:

  • Dropshipping is a specific E-Commerce model in which the seller does not keep products in stock.
  • Instead, when the seller receives an order, they purchase the product directly from a third-party supplier (usually wholesalers or manufacturers) and ship it directly to the customer.
  • This eliminates the need to maintain a large inventory, reducing operational costs and financial risks associated with inventory management.
  • Dropshipping allows entrepreneurs to focus more on marketing, sales, and customer service rather than dealing with storing and shipping products.

Both concepts are interrelated, and dropshipping can be a strategy used within the broader context of e-commerce. However, it's important to note that dropshipping also presents challenges, such as reliance on third parties for inventory and shipping, as well as a potentially lower profit margin compared to traditional E-Commerce models. Each approach has its advantages and disadvantages, and the choice between them will depend on the entrepreneur's goals and resources.

Average Salary: Potentially high, can range from $1000 to $10,000 per month.

10. Translation and Transcription Freelancer

A translation and transcription freelancer is a self-employed professional who offers translation and transcription services independently, rather than being linked to a company or organization full-time. These professionals work on their own, accepting projects from clients and charging for their services based on the work performed.

Translation:A translation freelancer is responsible for converting content from one language to another. This may include translating written documents, texts, websites, videos or any type of material that needs to be adapted into another language. It is crucial that the translator has in-depth knowledge of the source and target languages, as well as a solid understanding of cultural nuances to ensure an accurate and contextually appropriate translation.

Transcription:On the other hand, a transcription freelancer is tasked with converting audio or video into written text. This usually involves listening to recordings and transcribing the content word for word. Transcription freelancers need to have keen listening skills, good grammar and spelling, and be able to work accurately and efficiently.

These professionals can offer their services on specialized online platforms such as freelancers, where clients from all over the world can find and hire freelance translators and transcribers for their specific projects. The freelance model offers flexibility in work and allows professionals to choose the projects they want to take on, set their own prices and define their working hours.

Average Salary: $15-$40 per hour, depending on language and complexity.

11. Website and Blog Development

Website and blog development refers to the process of creating and building online platforms to share information, content and interact with users. This activity involves several steps, from initial planning to implementation and ongoing maintenance.

Here are some common steps in website and blog development:

  • Planning: The purpose of the website or blog, the target audience, the objectives are defined, and requirements are surveyed. The development platform and technologies to be used are also chosen.
  • Design: Creation of the visual structure and usability of the website. This includes defining layouts, color schemes, typography, and other visual elements.
  • Development: Actual implementation of the website or blog based on planning and design. Programming, coding and integration of functionalities, such as forms, commenting systems, content management systems (CMS), among others.
  • Content: Adding relevant and informative content, such as texts, images, videos, etc. This can be done manually or through a CMS that allows for easy content management.
  • Testing: Checking functionality, browser compatibility, mobile responsiveness and performance testing to ensure the website works correctly.
  • Hosting: Choosing a hosting service to make the website accessible on the internet. Hosting stores the website's files and provides the resources necessary for its operation.
  • Maintenance: Regular updates to ensure the security and proper functioning of the website. This may involve bug fixes, content updates, and implementing new features as needed.

For blogs, the focus may be more on textual content, but the principles of web development still apply. Many blogs are built on top of popular CMS platforms, like WordPress, which make content creation and management easier.

Average Salary: Initially, $25-$50 per hour; with experience, it can exceed $80 per hour.

12. Make Money with YouTube (AdSense and Partnerships)

Making money with YouTube involves several strategies and opportunities offered by the platform. Here are some common ways content creators can monetize their YouTube videos:

  • YouTube Partner Program (YPP): Creators can sign up for the YouTube Partner Program, which allows them to run ads on their videos. To qualify, you must meet requirements such as having more than 1,000 subscribers and 4,000 hours of watch time in the last 12 months.
  • Google AdSense Ads: Ads displayed on videos generate revenue for creators through the Google AdSense program, which is integrated with YPP.
  • Sponsorships and Partnerships: Creators can collaborate with brands for sponsorships and partnerships. This may involve promoting products or services during the video.
  • Product Sales and Merchandising: Many YouTubers sell products related to their brand, such as t-shirts, hats, books or digital products.
  • Super Chat and Super Stickers: During live streams, viewers can pay to feature their comments (Super Chat) or send stickers (Super Stickers), providing an additional way to generate revenue.
  • Affiliates: Creators can join affiliate programs, promoting products and earning a commission for each sale generated through their affiliate links.
  • Classes and Consulting: Some YouTubers offer online courses, consulting, or paid services related to their content niche.
  • YouTube Premium: Creators also receive a share of the revenue generated by YouTube Premium subscribers who watch their videos.

Remembering that financial success on YouTube often takes time and consistent effort in creating quality content, building an engaged audience, and exploring the various revenue streams available on the platform.

Average Salary: Variable, but successful YouTubers can earn anywhere from $1000 to millions per month.

Keep in mind that these numbers are averages and actual results may vary based on individual circumstances and dedication to the job.
